History of Siopao

Siopao is a cousin of the Chinese baozi (also known as bao), introduced by Hokkien Chinese immigrants to the Philippines during the period of Spanish colonization. Unlike bao, siopao is much larger and is usually eaten using the hands. It was popularized in the country by a Cantonese immigrant street vendor named Ma Mon Luk, who also created noodle soup in the 1920s.
