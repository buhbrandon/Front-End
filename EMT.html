Information about EMTs!

What is an EMT?

An emergency medical technician (EMT) is a medical professional that provides emergency medical services. EMTs are most commonly found serving on ambulances.

EMTs have emergency care skills, from bag-valve-mask ventilation and oxygen administration to the assessment and management of patients presenting with a spectrum of maladies.

Anyone can become an EMT! The age requirement is 16 years old to begin training.


EMTs are the second level of training on this section of emergency medical services (EMS).

The order of knowledge goes: EMR, EMT, AEMT, and Paramedic (Their courses are 800 hours long plus 600 hours of clinical time!).


How can I get certified?

Well first, you need to attend a rigorous class spanning around 190 hours. It has the workload equivalent to two college classes.

Being around other likeminded students is a great way to grow your love of the medical field. They all share your passion, so don't be afraid to talk about your interests!

After this, you will be authorized to take the NREMT certification exam! It is graded upon a pass/fail basis, meaning no percentages are involved. If you pass, you become an EMT.
